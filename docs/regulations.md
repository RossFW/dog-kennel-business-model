# Regulations & Licensing — Dog Kennel / Daycare at 4829 Peachtree Blvd

> There are three layers of compliance: **State (Georgia)**, **County (DeKalb)**, and **City (Chamblee)**. You need all three.

---

## Layer 1: State of Georgia — Georgia Department of Agriculture (GDA)

### Kennel License (Required)
Any establishment where dogs are boarded, held, trained, or cared for in exchange for payment must be licensed by the Georgia Department of Agriculture under the **Georgia Animal Protection Act (O.C.G.A. § 4-11-3)**.

**This applies to:** kennels, daycares, boarding facilities, training operations — all of it.

**License details:**
- Issued annually (12-month term)
- Fee: **$50–$400/year** based on holding capacity
  - Up to 20 animals: $100/year
  - Larger capacity: scales up, max $400/year
- Apply online via GDA's portal
- Contact: Companion Animal Program — (404) 656-4914 | CAECompliance@agr.georgia.gov | M–F 8am–4:30pm

### Inspection Process
1. Submit application online
2. GDA inspector conducts **pre-inspection** before license is issued
3. Inspector will identify any deficiencies and work with you to resolve them
4. License not issued until pre-inspection is passed
5. After opening: **inspections can be unannounced**, at whatever frequency the Commissioner deems necessary
6. Animal shelters: 2 mandatory annual inspections (kennels: less prescriptive, but still subject to unannounced visits)

### Mandatory Record-Keeping (O.C.G.A. § 4-11-3)
You must maintain records for every animal:
- Owner name, address, phone
- Animal type and quantity
- Incoming and outgoing dates
- Transfer recipient name, address, phone, date
- Veterinary visit dates and facility contact info
- Death dates and known causes

### GDA Facility Standards (Georgia Animal Protection Act Regulations)
Key facility requirements under Rule 40-13-13:
- **Ventilation**: Adequate air changes, no buildup of ammonia or noxious odors
- **Temperature**: Indoor temps maintained between 50°F–85°F; protection from weather extremes
- **Primary enclosures**: Structurally sound, kept clean, no sharp edges
- **Space per animal**: Enclosures must allow natural standing, turning, lying down — no cramped kennels
- **Sanitation**: Daily cleaning of enclosures; waste disposal
- **Lighting**: Adequate natural or artificial light
- **Exercise**: Dogs must have regular exercise opportunities (this is where daycare play yards matter)
- **Water**: Fresh, clean water at all times
- **Food**: Fed at least once daily; food stored to prevent contamination
- **Isolation**: Sick or injured animals must be isolated from healthy animals
- **Euthanasia**: Must be performed only by licensed vet or certified individual (not relevant for daycare/boarding, but in the statute)

## Layer 2: DeKalb County

### Business License
- Any commercial operation in DeKalb County must obtain a **DeKalb County Occupation Tax Certificate** (business license)
- Annual renewal
- Contact: DeKalb County Finance Department, Business License Division

### Animal Control Ordinance
DeKalb County has its own animal control ordinance that may impose additional requirements:
- Limits on number of animals on a property (varies by zoning)
- Noise ordinance (barking dogs can trigger complaints — plan mitigation upfront)
- Waste disposal regulations

### Health Department
- Commercial pet facilities may require a health department inspection
- Particularly relevant if you serve food/treats commercially or have a grooming component with water systems

## Layer 3: City of Chamblee — Zoning & Local Permits

### Zoning: VC (Village Commercial) — CRITICAL OPEN QUESTION

**The property is zoned VC (Village Commercial) by the City of Chamblee.**

The listing says VC "supports most Medical and Retail uses." However, **kennel/boarding is NOT automatically the same as retail or medical** — it may require a special use permit.

**What we know:** DogPhoria operates at 3571 Broad St, Chamblee, confirming that dog daycare/boarding IS operating in Chamblee. Whether their specific zoning differs from VC is unknown.

**Action required:** Call Chamblee Planning & Development to confirm:
- Is a dog kennel/boarding facility a permitted use by right in VC zoning?
- OR does it require a **Special Use Permit (SUP)**?
- Are there any size, noise, or operational restrictions?

**Contact:** City of Chamblee Planning & Development
- Phone: (770) 986-5010
- Address: 5468 Peachtree Rd, Chamblee GA 30341 (City Hall)
- Website: chambleega.com/planning_and_development

**Reference:** Chamblee Unified Development Ordinance (UDO), adopted June 16, 2015 — Chapter 210, Base Zoning Districts. Full text at library.municode.com/ga/chamblee.

### Building Permits
All buildout work requires City of Chamblee building permits:
- **Building permit**: structural changes, floor drain installation
- **Mechanical permit**: HVAC systems
- **Plumbing permit**: drains, water supply modifications
- **Electrical permit**: lighting, outlets, equipment
- **Fire inspection**: sprinkler systems, egress, occupancy rating for the animal count

### Certificate of Occupancy
After buildout, a new **Certificate of Occupancy (CO)** must be issued before you can operate. This involves fire marshal inspection and building inspection.

## Layer 4: Other Compliance Requirements

### Vaccination Requirements (Incoming Dogs)
Standard industry practice (and often required by your liability insurance):
- **Rabies** — required
- **Distemper/Parvovirus (DA2PP)** — required
- **Bordetella (kennel cough)** — required; especially important in group play settings
- **Canine Influenza** — increasingly required by premium facilities
- Documentation must be current and maintained on file

### Employee Requirements
- **Canine First Aid certification**: Not legally required in GA, but essential and expected
- **Background checks**: Recommended for all animal care staff
- **Rabies pre-exposure prophylaxis**: Georgia does not mandate this for kennel workers, but it is recommended by CDC for animal handlers
- No specific licensing required for individual handlers in GA (unlike some states)

### Insurance (Required and Critical)
- **General liability**: $1M–$2M minimum; covers dog bites, injuries to animals in your care, property damage
- **Care, Custody & Control (CCC) coverage**: Covers animals in your care specifically — standard GL may exclude this
- **Worker's compensation**: Required in Georgia if you have 3+ employees
- **Property insurance**: For the building and equipment
- Estimated annual cost: $20,000–$40,000/year depending on dog count capacity

### ADA Compliance
- If you have a public-facing retail/reception area, ADA accessibility requirements apply (ramps, door widths, restrooms)
- Service animals must always be admitted — cannot deny entry to handler's service animal

## Regulatory Timeline (Rough Sequence)

1. **Confirm VC zoning** → Chamblee Planning (1-2 days to confirm)
2. **Apply for SUP if needed** → Can take 30-90 days (public hearing process)
3. **Pull building permits** → After architectural drawings complete; 2-4 weeks
4. **Buildout** → 3-6 months
5. **Fire/building inspection** → After buildout complete
6. **Certificate of Occupancy** → Issued after passing inspections
7. **Apply for GDA Kennel License** → Pre-inspection required before opening
8. **DeKalb County business license** → Can apply concurrently
9. **OPEN**

**Realistic minimum timeline from decision to open: 8-12 months** (with no major zoning roadblocks).
