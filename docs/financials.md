## Capital Stack

### The Property Situation (Big Advantage)

**The family already owns this property.** The father is the current owner. This eliminates the single largest obstacle for most new business ventures: securing real estate.

| What this means | Impact |
|---|---|
| No mortgage | Saves ~$19,500/month vs. buying |
| No rent | Saves ~$26,700/month vs. leasing |
| Annual property taxes | ~$40,000/year (confirmed) |
| Property is a sunk cost | Family pays $40K/year regardless — any business use is pure upside |

**The business only needs to cover:**
1. Buildout / renovation capital (~$354K–$978K, estimate $600K)
2. Working capital for operations ramp (6-12 months of operating losses while building customers)
3. Ongoing property taxes: $40K/year (~$3,333/month — already in the expense model)

**Estimated total capital needed to open: $700K–$1.2M** (vs. $2M+ if buying or leasing a different space)

### Funding Options for Buildout
- Family equity / cash
- SBA 7(a) small business loan (business only, no real estate purchase needed)
- SBA 504 (if any construction involved)
- Local CDFI / small business grants (pet industry is eligible)
- Phased buildout: open daycare-only first (cheaper), add boarding later

## Unit Economics Snapshot

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Avg revenue per dog per day (daycare) | $40 |
| Variable cost per dog per day (est.) | ~$12 (labor + supplies) |
| **Gross margin per dog/day** | **~$28 (70%)** |
| Dogs needed to cover fixed costs ($1M) | ~98 dogs/day (@ $28 contribution) |
| Target daily census (50% capacity) | 40 dogs/day |
| Breakeven daily census | ~98 dogs |

**Interpretation:** Because the family owns the property, fixed costs drop dramatically. Instead of ~$320K/year in rent or ~$234K/year in mortgage, the annual property carry is just ~$40K. This is a massive structural advantage.

The thesis works if:
1. You can reach 40-50% utilization within 12-18 months (lower bar than a leased facility)
2. You add high-margin services (training, memberships, grooming) to drive revenue per customer
3. You manage the buildout budget tightly — the $600K in capex is now the main financial risk

---

## Critical Financial Risks

1. **Buildout capex**: Floor drains + dog-specific HVAC alone could hit $300K+. 1974 masonry slab = unknown problems. Get contractor bids before committing.
2. **Ramp period cash burn**: Building a customer base from zero takes 12-18 months. Need working capital reserve.
3. **Staffing**: Pet care labor is physically demanding and relatively low-paid. Turnover is a chronic industry problem. (See [docs/operations.md](operations.md))
4. **Opportunity cost**: If the property were leased to a commercial tenant, it could generate ~$320K/year in rent income. A dog kennel needs to clear that hurdle to be better than simply leasing the space.
