*Last updated: March 2026 — verified via Google Maps visual sweep + web research*

## Geospatial Overview

The property at 4829 Peachtree Blvd sits on a 35,000 VPD commuter corridor in Chamblee. We identified **~10 facility-based competitors** within 5 miles after a full visual map sweep. Key insight: **mileage is misleading in Atlanta**. At PM rush (5–7pm), a 4-mile competitor is a 30–45 minute drive — effectively a different market.

- **Immediate (0–2 mi):** DogPhoria, VCA (same road)
- **Brookhaven ring (3.8–4.5 mi, 25–40 min PM rush):** Puppy Haven, Dogtopia N. Brookhaven, Bark & Board
- **Dunwoody ring (4.5–5 mi, 25–35 min PM rush):** Camp Run-A-Mutt, Atlanta Pet Resort
- **Outer ring (6.5–9 mi, 45–70 min PM rush):** BHV Sandy Springs/Buckhead/Cheshire, Central Bark, Dogtopia Sandy Springs
- **Not direct competitors:** Jazzy Pawz (grooming only), Dillons Pet Club (grooming primarily), Dog River Retreat (Douglasville — different market)

## White Space — Verified Assessment

**The original broad claim ("no large competitor in Chamblee") was overfit. The nuanced truth:**

1. **Same-corridor monopoly**: DogPhoria (1 mile, 20 reviews) is the ONLY facility on the Peachtree Blvd corridor itself. Every other competitor is 3.8+ miles away and 25–45 minutes during drop-off/pickup hours.

2. **Sunday gap is real**: Every single competitor has limited or no Sunday hours. DogPhoria: 4–6pm only. Camp Run-A-Mutt: completely closed. Many others close at 4pm or have noon opens. A facility open 7am–7pm all 7 days would be differentiated.

3. **Scale gap**: 14,612 SF is 2–3× the floor plan of most competitors in 4,000–8,000 SF retail strips. Room for indoor large-dog/small-dog play separation, dedicated boarding wing, training room, grooming suite — competitors can't match this layout.

4. **Barking Hound Village gap**: 7 locations in Atlanta, zero in Chamblee/north DeKalb. They've intentionally or accidentally skipped this submarket.

5. **Price anchoring room**: $35/day (DogPhoria) is the local benchmark. Camp Run-A-Mutt charges $43/day. There is room to price $39–45/day and be competitive while capturing scale economies from larger dog counts.
