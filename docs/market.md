### What is CAGR?

**CAGR = Compound Annual Growth Rate.** It answers: *"If something grew from $X to $Y over N years, what consistent annual percentage growth rate does that represent?"*

**Formula:** `CAGR = (End Value ÷ Start Value)^(1 ÷ Years) - 1`

**Applied to our market:**
- 2024 value: $1.85 billion
- 2034 projected: $4.30 billion
- Years: 10
- CAGR = ($4.30B ÷ $1.85B)^(1/10) − 1 = (2.324)^(0.10) − 1 = **~8.6%**

**What "compounding" means in plain English:**

| Year | Market Size |
|---|---|
| 2024 (start) | $1.85B |
| 2025 | $2.01B (+8.6%) |
| 2026 | $2.18B (+8.6%) |
| 2027 | $2.37B (+8.6%) |
| ... | ... |
| 2034 | ~$4.30B |

Each year's growth builds on the previous year's larger base — like compound interest. A $1.85B market growing at 8.6%/year more than **doubles** in 10 years. Compare that to the S&P 500's long-run ~10% CAGR — this market is nearly keeping pace with the stock market. That's a healthy growth rate for a service business to enter.

## Demand Drivers

1. **Dual-income households**: Both partners working = dog needs supervision during the day. This is the core daycare demand driver.
2. **Work-from-office return**: Post-COVID RTO mandates (Amazon, major Atlanta employers) are pushing people back into offices → daycare demand up.
3. **Millennial/Gen Z pet ownership**: 33.6 median age in Chamblee = prime dog-owner demographic. Millennials are delaying children, treating pets as family.
4. **Travel recovery**: Boarding demand tied to travel. PDK airport adjacency is meaningful — business travelers need recurring boarding partners.
5. **Rising dog population in urban infill**: Chamblee and Brookhaven have seen significant new apartment/townhome development → more dogs, less yard space → more daycare need.

---

## Demand Risks

1. **Economic sensitivity**: Daycare/boarding is discretionary. In a recession, these are among the first cuts. (Though pet spend has historically been resilient.)
2. **Rover/Wag competition**: App-based in-home dog sitting at ~$25-35/day is real competition for price-sensitive customers. Facilities compete on socialization and professional oversight.
3. **Hispanic market nuance**: At 38.9% Hispanic, Chamblee's local demographic is underrepresented in premium pet service spending — culturally, pet boarding is less normalized in some communities. The real customer base skews toward higher-income commuters passing through on Peachtree Blvd.

## Key Question for Sizing

How many dogs/day can a 14,612 SF facility serve?

Industry benchmarks:
- ~75-100 SF of play space per dog (daycare)
- ~25-30 SF per kennel run (overnight boarding)
- If ~8,000 SF allocated to play space → **80-100 dogs/day daycare capacity**
- If ~3,000 SF to boarding → **100-120 kennels**

At $40/dog/day daycare, 80 dogs/day = **$3,200/day | ~$800K/year** (daycare alone, assuming 250 operating days).

This is a rough ceiling, not an assumption — occupancy rates, seasonality, and ramp-up matter enormously.
